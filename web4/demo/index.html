The Agent-as-Download

There is no app. You have an agent. Click the button, and your agent will negotiate with the provider, read its governance protocol, and generate an entire news experience — built for you, on the fly.

1

Agent Activation

A service worker registers in your browser — your agent's local presence. A few KB of JavaScript. That's the entire "download."

2

Protocol Handshake

Your agent contacts the provider and receives a governance protocol — rules, capabilities, design principles, forbidden patterns.

3

Negotiation

Your agent evaluates the governance protocol against your preferences. It decides what to accept, modify, or reject. Milliseconds.

4

Experience Generation

The provider's AI agent generates a complete HTML experience — custom-built for your preferences, your device, right now. No pre-built app. No template.

5

Your App Appears

The service worker serves the generated experience to your browser. It looks like an app. It behaves like an app. But it was never shipped — it was negotiated into existence.

What just happened?

When you clicked "Activate Agent," here's what actually occurred:

A service worker (a few KB of standard web JavaScript) registered in your browser. That service worker contacted the provider's endpoint and received a governance protocol — a JSON document declaring the provider's capabilities, design principles, and forbidden patterns (like infinite scroll, clickbait, and notification spam).

Your agent evaluated the governance protocol, then asked the provider's AI agent (Claude) to generate a complete HTML experience — styled, interactive, and personalized to your category preferences. The provider never shipped a frontend. It shipped rules and data. Your agent turned that into an app.

The generated experience is cached locally by the service worker. Reload it and it's instant — no server call. The "app" lives on your device now, built specifically for you.
