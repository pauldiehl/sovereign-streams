## Active Watch

### Tier 1: Directly Relevant

| Initiative | What They're Building | Why We Watch | Coalition Angle |
|------------|----------------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **[dp-web4](https://github.com/dp-web4/web4)** | Trust-based ecosystem proof of concept. Agent trust simulations. Commerce delegation with cryptographic enforcement. | Closest philosophical alignment to Coalition. Trust between agents and humans is their core focus. Small team, early stage. | High potential for collaboration or merge. Their trust simulation work could validate our self-sovereign trust model empirically. Reach out. |
| **[Holochain](https://www.holochain.org/)** | Agent-centric distributed computing. No blockchain, no global consensus. Each agent maintains local state with mutual accountability. Cryptographic audit trails. | Philosophy is almost identical: agent sovereignty, individual and collective autonomy honored, mutual accountability without central authority. Mature framework (since 2018). | Their agent-centric architecture maps to self-sovereign trust. [hREA](https://hrea.io/) (economic coordination layer built on Holochain) is directly relevant to Coalition economics. Infrastructure partner, not competitor. |
| **[Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF)](https://openai.com/index/agentic-ai-foundation/)** | Open standards for agent interoperability under Linux Foundation. Founded Dec 2025 by Anthropic, OpenAI, Block. Houses MCP (Model Context Protocol), Goose, AGENTS.md specification. | Provides the protocol rails agents need to communicate. MCP is becoming the standard for agent-tool connectivity. AGENTS.md could be how agents discover governance protocols. | Use their standards. Don't reinvent protocols when AAIF is building them with massive corporate backing. Coalition's value is the trust model and radical generosity — not the wire protocol. |
| **[AGNTCY](https://agntcy.org/)** | Agent discovery, identity, secure messaging, observability. Originally Cisco-led under Linux Foundation. 65+ companies including Google, Dell, Red Hat. | Agent discovery is the exchange problem. Identity is the "don't let agents dress up as other agents" problem. Both core to Coalition infrastructure needs. | Monitor closely. Their discovery protocol could become the backbone of how Coalition agents find each other. Identity verification (separate from trust) maps directly to our self-sovereign trust model. |

## What We're Looking For

When scanning for new additions to this watchlist:

**Green flags (potential Coalition allies):**
- Agent-centric (not platform-centric) architecture
- Open protocols without corporate gatekeeping
- Trust as earned (not enforced)
- Giving / open-source culture beyond just code
- P2P economics over platform extraction
- Small teams with big vision (Milliprimes)

**Red flags (watch but keep distance):**
- "Trustless" as a feature (Web 3.0 thinking)
- Centralized trust authority
- Mandatory certification / compliance gatekeeping
- Token speculation as primary value proposition
- Corporate consortium where "open" means "open to members who pay"
